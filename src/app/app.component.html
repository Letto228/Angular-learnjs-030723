#1 Angular's ‹ng-template› element defines a template that is not rendered by default.

Why ng-template rules:

  • You have full control over how and when the content is displayed.
  • You define template content that is only being rendered by Angular when you, instruct it to do so.
  • You can display copies of ‹ng-template› content by means of structural directives
  • Inside the ‹ng-template› tags you can reference variables present in the surrounding outer template
